📡 WSJT-Y User Guide

WSJT-Y is 2E0LXY's independent fork of WSJT-Z (SQ9FVE), itself built on WSJT-X (K1JT and the WSJT-X Development Group). This guide covers everything specific to WSJT-Y — the map, roster, band automation, remote control, and the rest of what's been added on top of the core FT8/FT4/WSPR/MSK144 engine WSJT-X already provides.

🖥️ Main Window Layout

PanelWhat it's for

Call RosterTwo-column live table of everyone currently on the band. See below.
Every panel above is a dock — drag its title bar to reposition, or right-click / use its hamburger menu to pin left, pin right, or float it. Closed a panel by accident? It's still in the View menu.

📻 Band & Frequency Control

The band toolbar along the top gives one-click access to every FT8 calling frequency, 160m through 2m. Click a band button to QSY immediately.

Auto Band Hop

Tick Auto Hop and WSJT-Y checks every 5 minutes whether a better band is available, based on time of day, season, and a propagation table (D-layer absorption at night favours the low bands; F2-layer and equinox conditions favour the high bands; a rough Sporadic-E season allowance for 12m/10m). It only moves bands — it never touches your Tx settings.

Auto Hop is a heuristic, not a real-time propagation feed — treat a hop as "worth a look", not a guarantee. Turn it off any time you want to stay put.

📞 Working Stations

Double-click a decode in Band ActivityQSYs to their frequency, fills in their call/grid, generates the reply sequence.

Auto CQ / Auto Call

Auto CQ calls CQ on a repeating cycle. Auto Call answers CQs automatically — turning it on locks CQ only on (shown dimmed/green) so you're only ever auto-answering genuine CQ callers, not partway-through exchanges.

If CQ only looks stuck on and won't untick, check whether Auto Call is on — that's the lock, not a bug.

Watch for Callsign

Type a callsign into the Call Roster's watch for callsign field. When it's heard: the row highlights yellow, a notification pops up, WSJT-Y beeps, then it QSYs, fills in the call, and starts calling automatically — the same sequence as a map double-click.

🗺️ DX Station Map

  • Pan / zoom: left-drag to pan, scroll wheel to zoom, home button to reset.
  • Pin left / right / float: hamburger menu on the map, or right-click its dock title.
  • Greyline: the soft night-side shading is the real-time day/night terminator, computed from your system clock — a classic guide to likely enhanced propagation paths at dawn/dusk.
  • Station info panel: click a dot to see grid, distance/bearing, DXCC, continent, CQ/ITU zone, and (if configured) their QRZ.com photo and email — all in one place below the map.

📋 Call Roster

Two side-by-side tables so more stations fit on screen at once. Click any column header to sort — click again to reverse. The bar along the bottom shows the most distant callsign, the strongest signal, and the weakest, across everything currently listed.

Double-clicking Erase clears the roster too, alongside the decode history — handy for a clean slate at the start of a new band/session.

🔍 Filtering & Band Activity

The Filtering / Prefixes / States / Ignore / Alerts / Others tab strip above Band Activity controls what gets shown or ignored: DXCC entity filters, US state filters, a personal ignore list, and audible/visual alerts for callsigns or countries you care about. The CQ only button above Band Activity is the quickest single toggle if all you want is to hide non-CQ traffic.

📗 Logging & QRZ

QSOs log the normal way (Log QSO button, or automatically depending on your settings) — nothing about the map, roster, or remote app changes how logging works; they all just read from the same log. QRZ.com lookups (photo, name, email, callbook grid) populate the map's info panel when configured with a QRZ XML subscription under Settings.

A station's QRZ.com callbook grid is separate from the grid they actually transmitted on air — WSJT-Y always uses the on-air decoded grid for distance/bearing, never the callbook one, since hams don't always keep their QRZ profile current.

📱 Remote Control (Android App)

The companion Android app shows the live decode stream, lets you tap a CQ to reply, change band, and halt Tx — from your phone. It does not keep its own log; every QSO is still logged by WSJT-Y itself, exactly as normal.

Setup

  1. In WSJT-Y: Tools → Configure Remote Control.
  2. Set a password — the same one goes into the Android app. Required either way; leaving it blank disables remote access entirely rather than leaving it open.
  3. Choose one connection mode (or both):
    ModeHowTrade-off
    RelayEnter a wss:// relay address (see the wsjty-relay project). Works anywhere with no router changes. Needs a relay server running somewhere; adds one extra hop.
    DirectEnter a port; WSJT-Y listens directly. On the same WiFi it just works; over the internet, forward that port on your router. Plain ws:// — not encrypted in transit. Fine on a trusted LAN; a real trade-off if you expose it over WAN.

🌐 PSK Reporter

Spots your decodes to the PSK Reporter network when enabled under Settings. The PSK Reporter window only opens when you ask for it (Decode menu) — it no longer reopens itself on every launch.

💡 Tips

  • Collapse the Gen Msgs / Tx editor (small arrow above Band Activity) when you don't need it — Band Activity reclaims the space automatically.
  • The band toolbar, Call Roster, and DX Station Map are all separate docks — arrange them once to suit your monitor and WSJT-Y remembers the layout.
  • Greyline plus a quick glance at Auto Band Hop's suggestion is a fast way to decide where to point your beam.

⚠️ Do's and Don'ts

✅ Do❌ Don't
Keep your callsign, grid, and rig settings accurate in Configuration before operating. Leave Auto Call running unattended for long periods without checking in — you're still responsible for what your station transmits.
Set a real password before enabling remote control, on both ends. Expose direct mode over WAN without understanding it's unencrypted transport.
Double-check band edges and your licence's power/mode privileges before transmitting. Assume Auto Band Hop's suggestion overrides your own judgement about propagation or band plans.

🛠️ Troubleshooting

SymptomLikely cause
CQ only won't untickAuto Call is on and has locked it — turn off Auto Call first.
PSK Reporter shows "TLS initialization failed"Missing OpenSSL runtime library on Linux (fixed by the packaged .deb's dependencies from v3.4.10 on) — if it persists, check your system has a current libssl installed.
Remote app won't connectPassword must match exactly on both ends. For direct mode, confirm the port is actually reachable (same WiFi, or forwarded correctly on your router).
Map markers look wrong for a stationThe map always plots the decoded on-air grid, not a QRZ profile grid — if a station's transmission didn't include a grid, no marker will show until one is heard.
